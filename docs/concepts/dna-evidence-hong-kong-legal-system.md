# DNA Evidence in Hong Kong Legal System

## Overview

DNA evidence represents a revolutionary advancement in Hong Kong's criminal justice system, fundamentally transforming how crimes are investigated and prosecuted. The Tuen Mun Rapist case marked the first successful use of DNA evidence in Hong Kong, establishing a precedent that has since been adopted across the territory's legal framework.

## Historical Context

### The Watershed Moment
The Tuen Mun Rapist case involving Lam Kwok Wai (林國偉) stands as a landmark case in Hong Kong's legal history:

**First DNA Case**
- **Significance**: Hong Kong's first case successfully solved using DNA evidence
- **Impact**: Revolutionized criminal investigation methods
- **Legal Precedent**: Established new standards for forensic evidence admissibility
- **Public Trust**: Enhanced public confidence in scientific evidence

### Case Background
The case involved a serial sexual offender who evolved from sexual assault to murder, with investigators successfully using DNA technology to identify and convict the perpetrator.
