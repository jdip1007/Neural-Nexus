# Penn Rainford

## Overview

Penn F. Rainford is a researcher at the intersection of computer science and knowledge management, focusing on sustainable scientific research practices and knowledge preservation strategies in the era of big science and artificial intelligence.

## Key Facts

- **Current Position**: Department of Computer Science, University of York
- **Secondary Role**: Research IT, IT Services, University of York
- **Research Focus**: Knowledge preservation, reproducibility, sustainable scientific research
- **Collaboration**: Works extensively with Thomas E. Gorochowski at University of Bristol
- **Publication**: Lead author on "Knowledge preservation in the era of big science and AI: strategies for sustainable scientific research" in Nature Communications (2026)

## Research Interests

### Knowledge Management
- Development of sustainable strategies for preserving scientific knowledge
- Addressing the reproducibility crisis through improved documentation practices
- Building digital infrastructure for long-term knowledge accessibility

### Big Science and AI
- Impact of artificial intelligence on scientific research practices
- Challenges of scale in modern scientific research
- Integration of computational and experimental approaches

### Interdisciplinary Research
- Bridging computer science with life sciences
- Cross-disciplinary knowledge sharing and preservation
- Community-driven standards for research documentation

## Key Contributions
