## Detailed Notes

### eDNA Overview

Environmental DNA (eDNA) consists of genetic material shed by organisms into their environment, including skin cells, feces, urine, and other biological materials that can be detected in water samples through molecular analysis.

### Method Components

#### Sample Collection
- **Water Filtration**: Large volume water processing
- **Filter Preservation**: Proper storage and transport conditions
- **Field Processing**: Immediate stabilization of samples
- **Contamination Prevention**: Sterile techniques maintained

#### DNA Extraction
- **Cell Capture**: Filtration and centrifugation methods
- **Lysis Optimization**: Specialized buffers for environmental samples
- **Inhibitor Removal**: Addressing environmental contaminants
- **Concentration**: Enrichment of low-concentration DNA

#### Quality Control
- **Extraction Efficiency**: Assessment of DNA recovery
- **Contamination Monitoring**: Prevention of false positives
- **Sample Integrity**: Preservation of sample quality
- **Analytical Validation**: Confirmation of method reliability
